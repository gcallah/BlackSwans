Chester spotted Ricco as soon as he entered the bar. Not that doing so was much of an accomplishment – given the man's shaved head, his protruding goatee, his multiple tattoos, a belly that looked as if it held a whole, undigested watermelon inside and which was embellished with a herniated navel that added two inches to its girth, and the glittering hoops of gold hanging prominently from his ears, Ricco was pretty easy to spot. He was playing darts towards the rear of the main room, so Chester ordered a beer, and then wandered past the dartboard, greeting Ricco and giving him what he hoped was a discrete nod meant to convey that he had come on business. Chester stopped to watch two guys playing pool in the back room before exiting onto the patio beyond to smoke a cigarette.

Chester had taken only a couple of puffs when he heard Ricco loudly asking, "Did anyone see where the nutty professor went?" He stuck his head back into the light of the bar and waved to catch Ricco's eye.

Ricco weaved rearward through the clumps of patrons to meet Chester outside. "Listen, professor, I don't have anything right now, but I want to take care of my boy, so I'm going to call a friend."

With a flourish he produced a cellphone from the pocket of his baggy shorts and poked at its buttons with his thick fingers. He held it expectantly to his ear. After a few seconds, he said, "Yo, niggah, I'm at the Blue Moon. I need you to get here right away."

He shook his head in disgust at the reply from the other party. "No, bitch, fifteen minutes ain't gonna cut it--you better get your ass here in ten." Then he snapped his phone shut. "Fifteen minutes," he informed Chester with a grin.

"Fifteen minutes is fine." Chester dropped the stub of his fag onto the cement at his feet and stamped it out. "I'll be inside."

He had only slept for three or four hours the night before, and had spent most of that day walking around town. He felt like he was about to collapse, so he found a niche to occupy on one of the three leather couches near the bar. As he scanned the scene around him he noticed a young, well-dressed couple, whom he guessed had only been dating briefly, sitting on the sofa directly opposite and perhaps ten feet away from his. They were deep in conversation, but they looked up when Ricco appeared between the two couches a moment later, shouting a drink order to the bartender. As Ricco waited for her to pour his beer, he turned to Chester and proclaimed, "I've gotta start a trial on Wednesday. I'm looking at ten fucking years. What really sucks is that I didn't even fucking do it. Shit, if I had popped somebody off"--here he paused to make a shooting gesture--"I could do the time standing on my head, but I gotta take a fall for some other shithead." Chester noted that the couple's conversation had been totally de-railed, and they both were staring at Ricco while trying to pretend that they simply had suddenly become enraptured by the deer head mounted on the wall above this--apparently homicidal--maniac's shoulder.

Chester rose from the leather cushions to get another beer himself. After he had ordered, he told Ricco, "That does suck. What's the charge?"

"Nah, you don't even wanna know." Ricco took a gulp from his mug. "Hey, how's your new book coming?"

"I should be done in a few months."

"You're gonna bring me a copy, right?"

"Of course."

"I want it autographed."

"No problem."

"That way, I can waste you after you give it to me, and get a lot of dough for it."

Chester chuckled. Glancing over at the pair of eavesdroppers, he felt certain that they had decided they had come to the wrong bar. He stared off at the ceiling as if seriously evaluating Ricco's idea, and then said, in a deadpan voice, "Not a bad plan."

Ricco wasn't quite sure what to make of that response. He put his beefy hands on Chester's shoulders, shook them, and said, "You know I'm just messing with you, don't you? I would never do wrong by my boy."

Chester laughed. "Of course I know. And anyway, I'd fuck you up before you even got a shot off."

Ricco loved this, and put an arm over Chester's shoulder. Chester looked across the room, but their audience had fled.
